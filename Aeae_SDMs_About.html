Aedes aegypti Suitability in Haiti

Modeling current and future distributions under climate change scenarios

Project Overview

This project models the distribution of Aedes aegypti mosquitoes across Haiti under current climate conditions and projects how suitable habitat may expand under global warming scenarios of +1.5°C, +2°C, +3°C, and +4°C. Aedes aegypti is the primary vector for dengue virus, Zika virus, chikungunya virus, and yellow fever virus, all pathogens of past or present public health importance in Haiti.

Key Finding: More than 8 million people in Haiti (44.85% of the population) currently live in areas with moderate risk of encountering Aedes aegypti. Under 4°C of warming, we project a dramatic increase to more than 95% of the population.

Key Results

Current Distribution

28.6% of Haiti's landscape suitable (≥0.5 probability)
8.0M people at moderate risk
479K internally displaced people at moderate risk

Climate Change Projections

Under warming scenarios, suitable habitat for Aedes aegypti is projected to expand dramatically across Haiti:

  • +1.5°C warming: Suitable area increases to 52% of landscape on average
  • +2°C warming: Suitable area reaches 60% of landscape
  • +3°C warming: Suitable area expands to 75% of landscape
  • +4°C warming: Suitable area covers 86% of landscape

Geographic Patterns

At baseline, the North-East Department showed the highest suitability (48.7% of the landscape suitable at least moderately suitable), followed by the North-West Department (mean = 0.41).

The two Communes with highest baseline suitability were Caracol (mean = 0.84) and Delmas (mean = 0.83).

About the Modeling Approach

This analysis used ensemble Boosted Regression Tree (BRT) models based on 60 presence locations obtained from the VectorMap database and 19 bioclimatic variables from WorldClim 2.1 (at ~1km² resolution). Thirty model replicates were generated, with pseudo-absence points placed at least 717 meters from known presence locations (based on the species' maximum flight range described by Verdonschot and Besse-Lotoskaya in 2014).

Future climate projections were derived from 28 global climate models from CMIP6, screened to exclude models with transient climate response values outside IPCC-recommended ranges. The most influential variables for predicting Aedes aegypti distribution were annual mean temperature, maximum temperature of the warmest month, and temperature seasonality.

Data Sources

  • Mosquito Occurrences: 60 presence locations from VectorMap database (vectormap.si.edu)
  • Climate Data: WorldClim 2.1 (30 arc-second resolution, ~1km²)
  • Future Climate: CMIP6 warming level ensembles (28 GCMs)
  • Population Data: Gridded Population of the World v4 (2020)
  • Internally Displaced Persons: International Organization for Migration's Displacement Tracking Matrix (2024)

Public Health Implications

These findings highlight the importance of understanding vector distributions in resource-constrained settings like Haiti. The projected expansion of suitable habitat under climate change represents an issue of global health equity, especially given the number of internally displaced people across Haiti's landscape.

Under 4°C of warming, suitable habitat is projected to expand into previously unsuitable areas in the Centre and Grand'Anse Departments, potentially exposing populations with limited vector control infrastructure to new disease transmission risks.
